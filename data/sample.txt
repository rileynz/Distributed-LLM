This is a small sample text file for training the tiny distributed
language model. It is just placeholder text written for this project
so that train.py has something to learn from right out of the box.

The model is very small, so it will not learn deep meaning from a tiny
file like this one. What it can learn from even a short, repetitive
text is the basic shape of language: which letters tend to follow
which other letters, where spaces and punctuation usually go, and
common short words like "the", "and", "is", and "a".

For a model that produces genuinely interesting text, replace this
file with a much larger piece of text of your own choosing. Good
options include any public domain book from Project Gutenberg
(gutenberg.org), your own writing, transcripts of things you like, or
any other plain text file. Aim for at least a few hundred kilobytes of
text for noticeably better results than this short sample gives.

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. The quick brown fox jumps
over the lazy dog again and again, because repetition is exactly the
kind of pattern that a tiny character-level model can actually learn
to predict, even with very little training data and very little time.
